Plagiarism

Plagiarism is the "wrongful appropriation" and "stealing and publication" of another author's "language, thoughts, ideas, or expressions" and the representation of them as one's own original work. (Wikipedia, 2014)

Plagiarism is a serious academic offence and is an unacceptable practice in professional life and at all levels of studies. The following are the consequences for a student caught the first time plagiarizing work for a hand-in assignment:

  1. A zero mark on the original assignment.
  2. A note or phone call to parents.
  3. A rewritten assignment has to be resubmitted within 24 hours. The assignment has to be signed by a parent.
  4. The new assignment will be marked out of a 50% of the original maximum score, e.g. if the plagiarized assignment was out of 10 points the mark on the resubmitted assignment will be out 5 points.
Note that these consequences are only for first time offences. Students found plagiarizing a second time (or more) will automatically get a zero on the assignment and their parents will be called in for a meeting with the teacher.
