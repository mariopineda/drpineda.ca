More on pressure... Jennifer notices that she sinks in the snow when walking in her snow boots, but she stays near the surface of the snow when walking in her snowshoes. The force exerted on the snow by Jeniffer's mass is 600 N. The surface area of her sole of one of her snow boots is 1000 `\( \mbox{cm}^2 \)`, and the surface area of one of her snowshoes is 2800 `\( \mbox{cm}^2 \)`. Compare the pressure Jennifer exerts on the snow with her snow boots and with her snowshoes. Use the calculation results to expplain the reason her snowshoes prevent her from sinking in the snow. --- # Solution Pressure exerted in snow boots: $$P=\frac{F}{A} = \frac{\mbox{Force exerted by Jennifer's mass}}{\mbox{Surface area of snow boot}}$$ $$P=\frac{600~\mbox{N}}{1000~\mbox{cm}^2} = 0.600~\mbox{N}/\mbox{cm}^2$$ Pressure exerted in snowshoes: $$P=\frac{F}{A} = \frac{\mbox{Force exerted by Jennifer's mass}}{\mbox{Surface area of snowshoes}}$$ $$P=\frac{600~\mbox{N}}{2800~\mbox{cm}^2} = 0.214~\mbox{N}/\mbox{cm}^2$$ --- # Solution (cont.) Comparing the pressures: $$\frac{\mbox{Pressure exerted in snow boots}}{\mbox{Pressure exerted in snowshoes}} = \frac{0.600}{0.214} = 2.8$$ The pressure Jennifer exerts on the snow in snow boots is `\( 2.8 \times \)` greater than the pressure she exerts in snowshoes. By reducing the pressure she exererts, the greater surface area of snowshoes prevents Jennifer from sinking into the snow. --- layout: false class: center, middle, inverse #...and now over to something else! --- # Lab: Compression of Liquids and Gases - p81 in textbook - Groups of 3 - Division of labour: - syringe operator (With a water-filled syringe, comes great responsibility) - ruler operator (have to be quick and accurate) - data recorder (has to be organized and meticulous) - Different groups will use different sized syringes - Follow instructions in textbook (omit step 7) ## Procedure 1.
