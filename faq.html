--- layout: page permalink: /faq/index.html title: FAQ description: "" tags: image: feature: --- Sometimes I get asked questions. Here are some of the more common ones in no particular order.

Why are you becoming a teacher?

After almost two decades as a researcher in academia, government and industry I reached a point in my career where I realized that as much as I love doing science my true passion is in teaching and sharing my excitement and love for science, engineering and mathematics with children and youth. Once I realized where my true passion was the decision to take my career to the next level was a straightforward decision.

Why did you leave academia?

Academia is an unique place for pushing the boundaries of what we know, what we can do and to pursue your curiosity in whatever direction it takes you. I was fortunate enough to have the opportunity to be a researcher in academia for almost two decades. I retired from academic research in 2012 in order to take my career to the next level by pursuing a teaching degree. See question above as to why I decided to become a teacher?

Can you give a classroom presentation in my class?

I would be happy to. I currently give classroom presentations in the Edmonton area on the grade 6 Trees & Forests Unit. To request a presentation please use the ESON booking procedures.

Can I join one of the FIRST robotics team that you coach?

I am coaching several FIRST robotics team for different age groups (JrFLL for ages 6-9, FLL for ages 10-14 and FRC for ages 15-18). If you live in Edmonton and are interested in joining one of the robotics teams have your parents contact me.

Can I use/repost one of your classroom resources and/or a blog post?

Unless otherwise stated, all content on this site is licensed under a Creative Commons License, specifically CC BY-NC-SA 3.0. Although this is a permissive license make sure you understand what it entails before reposting or remixing any material. You can find more info here. If in doubt feel free to contact me.

Can you send me a reprint of one of your research papers?

Assuming I have a PDF version of the paper I would be happy to. Make your request at my contact page. For a somewhat complete list of my publications see my Google Scholar page.

Can you review a paper/book chapter/grant, etc?

Due to other commitments I am no longer able to review any academic material.
