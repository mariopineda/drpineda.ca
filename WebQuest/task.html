It is the fall of 2011 and Prime Minister Stephen Harper has announced that the Canadian Government will withdraw its support for the Kyoto Protocol. You are a scientist at a Canadian research institution doing research on climate change. You have been asked by the opposition to attend a senate hearing in the House of Commons to present evidence on climate change in a last-ditch attempt to reverse the governments decision. The scientific evidence you will present at the hearing will be pivotal in determining the future role Canada will play in stemming the tide of antrophomorphic climate change.

Working in pairs choose one of the scientific fields below. Your task is to pretend that you are researchers in the scientific discipline you have chosen. Your research and presentation should be focused on the research topic described under the discipline specifically addressing how climate change will affect the biotic and abiotic aspects of the ecological and/or environmental system you are researching. You will present your findings to the House of Commons (i.e. your class) and the Prime Minister (your teacher) during a poster session. You will have two classes to conduct your research, one class for organizing and creating the poster, and you will present your poster on the fourth day.

Plant ecology

You are plant ecologists studying how climate change affects the phenology of plants and animals.

Animal ecology

You are ecologists studying how climate change affects polar bears in Hudson Bay.

Oceanography

You are oceanographers studying how climate change affects sea level changes and currents in the South Pacific.

Meteorology

You are meterologists researching how climate change affects current and future global weather patterns.

Glaciology

You are glaciologists studying how climate change affects glaciers in Banff National Park.

Forest entomology

You are forest entomologists studying the role climate change has on outbreaks of mountain pine beetles in Alberta.

Limnology

You are limnologist working at the Experimental Lakes Area studying how climate change affects freshwater systems.

Geochemistry

You are geochemists researching the effects of increased atmospheric carbon dioxide levels on ocean pH, global carbon cycle, and its impacts on organisms.

Medicine

You are medical doctors studying how climate change will affect the global prevalence of infectious diseases.
